Definitions of Degrees of Performance

  1. Unsatisfactory performance does not meet the requirements of the position.
  2. Needs Improvement performance is marginal. Employee requires constant supervision. Barely meets minimum standards.
  3. Satisfactory performance is that which meets the requirements of the position. The position is being covered in an adequate manner and the responsibilities are being handled adequately.
  4. Good performance is marked by initiative and high quality and quantity of work. Employee regularly makes valuable contributions to the organization. Judgments are sound and employee demonstrates knowledge and mastery of his/her position.
  5. Outstanding performance is consistently characterized by exceptionally high quality work that leaves little or nothing to be desired. Employee repeatedly makes contributions to the organization which are far above the requirements of his/her position.

If a rating of 1 or 5 is selected, a written explanation is required in the provided text field.
